Hepatitis A

Most destinations

Spread via contaminated food and water. Recommended for most travel outside northern and western Europe, North America and Australasia.

From £55 per dose

Hepatitis B

Higher-risk travel

Recommended for those with potential blood exposure, longer stays, or working in healthcare. Often combined with Hepatitis A (Twinrix).

From £60 per dose

Rabies

Adventure travel

Essential for adventure travel, rural areas and destinations with limited post-exposure treatment. Pre-exposure course is 3 doses over 21+ days.

From £75 per dose

Cholera

High-risk areas

Dukoral oral vaccine recommended for humanitarian workers, relief agencies and those visiting cholera-affected areas with poor sanitation.

From £50 per dose

Japanese Encephalitis

Rural Asia

Recommended for travellers spending a month or more in rural parts of Asia, particularly near rice paddies or pig farms where the virus circulates.

From £120 per dose

Required for Hajj

Mandatory for Hajj and Umrah pilgrims. Also recommended for travel to the sub-Saharan "meningitis belt" during the dry season.

The process

From booking to boarding pass —
here's how it works

Most travel vaccinations can be completed in a single visit. We recommend booking at least 4–6 weeks before departure, though we'll do everything we can for late bookings too.

1 Book

Book your appointment

Common questions

Travel vaccination FAQs

Everything you need to know before you book.

We recommend booking at least 4–6 weeks before departure. Some vaccines require multiple doses spread over several weeks (e.g. Rabies takes 3 doses over 21 days minimum), and some need time to become fully effective. That said, even if you're travelling in under a week, please call us — partial courses still offer meaningful protection and we'll do everything possible to help.
It depends on your destination. Many countries in sub-Saharan Africa and South America require proof of Yellow Fever vaccination for entry — without it, you may be refused boarding or quarantined on arrival. Some countries require it only if you're arriving from a Yellow Fever-endemic region. As a designated NHS Yellow Fever Vaccination Centre, we issue the official International Certificate of Vaccination or Prophylaxis (ICVP) — the only document accepted at borders — valid for life from 10 days after vaccination.
Required vaccines are mandatory for entry into certain countries — Yellow Fever and Meningitis ACWY (for Hajj/Umrah) are the main examples. You will need documented proof. Recommended vaccines aren't a legal requirement but are strongly advised based on the health risks at your destination. We follow live NATHNAC guidance to give you destination-specific, up-to-date recommendations — not generic checklists.
Some vaccines are available free on the NHS — notably Tetanus/Diphtheria/Polio (Td/IPV) boosters, Hepatitis B (in certain circumstances), and MMR if you're not up to date. Most travel vaccines — including Yellow Fever, Typhoid, Hepatitis A, Rabies, and antimalarials — are private services that must be paid for. We'll always tell you which vaccines may be available on the NHS before recommending private alternatives.
Yes. Our pharmacists are independent prescribers and can issue private prescriptions for Malarone (atovaquone/proguanil), Doxycycline, and Mefloquine. The right antimalarial depends on your destination, the specific malaria strain present, your medical history, and how you tolerate each drug. We'll go through all of this during your consultation and prescribe the most appropriate option.
Some vaccines are safe in pregnancy (e.g. inactivated vaccines like Hepatitis A, Typhoid, and Flu), while others — particularly live vaccines like Yellow Fever — require careful clinical assessment. Our pharmacists are trained to advise on vaccine safety during pregnancy and breastfeeding and will review the benefit-risk balance with you individually. Please mention your pregnancy or breastfeeding status when booking.
Call your nearest branch as soon as possible. Even with just a few days until departure, most single-dose vaccines can be given on the same day and will offer some protection — a partial course is always better than nothing. We'll also prioritise the vaccines that matter most for your specific destination so your time and budget are used wisely.
Yes. Every patient receives a written vaccination record covering all vaccines administered, with dates and batch numbers. For Yellow Fever, we issue the official ICVP (International Certificate of Vaccination or Prophylaxis) — the yellow booklet accepted at borders worldwide. Keep this document safe; losing it may require re-vaccination to obtain a replacement certificate.
