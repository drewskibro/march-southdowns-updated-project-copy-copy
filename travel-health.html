What We Offer

Everything you need before you fly

From your first consultation to the certificate in your hand — Southdowns covers every part of your travel health journey.

Official Centre · NATHNAC Registered

Yellow Fever Vaccination & ICVP Certificate

As an official NATHNAC-registered Yellow Fever Centre, Southdowns Pharmacy can administer the vaccine and issue the internationally recognised International Certificate of Vaccination or Prophylaxis (ICVP) — required for entry to 42+ countries.

Travel Vaccinations

Full range of destination-specific travel vaccines including Typhoid, Hepatitis A & B, Rabies, Cholera, Japanese Encephalitis, Meningitis, and more — all in stock, no waiting.

Malaria Prevention

Destination-specific antimalarial medication — Malarone, Doxycycline, or Mefloquine — prescribed and dispensed by our pharmacists based on your exact travel itinerary and medical history.

Pre-Travel Health Advice

Personalised guidance on food and water safety, insect bite prevention, altitude sickness, sun protection, and travel-specific health risks — tailored to your destination and activities.

Travel Health Kits

Destination-specific first aid and health supplies assembled by your pharmacist — from rehydration sachets and insect repellent to sterile equipment kits for high-risk destinations.

Post-Travel Follow-Up

Concerned about symptoms after you return? Our pharmacists can screen for delayed-onset conditions including fever, rashes, and gastrointestinal illness, and refer you promptly if needed.

Expert Consultation

Not sure what you need? Book a 30-minute travel health consultation and our GPhC-registered pharmacist will build a complete health plan around your specific itinerary.

Mandatory for entry to many countries in sub-Saharan Africa and South America. As an NHS-approved Yellow Fever Vaccination Centre, we can issue the official International Certificate of Vaccination or Prophylaxis (ICVP) — the only document accepted at borders.

Hepatitis A

Most destinations

Spread via contaminated food and water. Recommended for most travel outside northern and western Europe, North America and Australasia.

From £55 per dose

Hepatitis B

Higher-risk travel

Recommended for those with potential blood exposure, longer stays, or working in healthcare. Often combined with Hepatitis A (Twinrix).

From £60 per dose

Rabies

Adventure travel

Essential for adventure travel, rural areas and destinations with limited post-exposure treatment. Pre-exposure course is 3 doses over 21+ days.

From £75 per dose

Cholera

High-risk areas

Dukoral oral vaccine recommended for humanitarian workers, relief agencies and those visiting cholera-affected areas with poor sanitation.

From £50 per dose

Japanese Encephalitis

Rural Asia

Recommended for travellers spending a month or more in rural parts of Asia, particularly near rice paddies or pig farms where the virus circulates.

From £120 per dose

Meningitis ACWY

Required for Hajj

Mandatory for Hajj and Umrah pilgrims. Also recommended for travel to the sub-Saharan "meningitis belt" during the dry season.

From £65 per dose

The process

From booking to boarding pass —
here's how it works

Most travel vaccinations can be completed in a single visit. We recommend booking at least 4–6 weeks before departure, though we'll do everything we can for late bookings too.

1 Book

Book your appointment

Book online or call your nearest branch. Choose a time that suits you — evening and Saturday slots available at most locations.

2 Consult

Travel health consultation

Your GPhC-registered pharmacist reviews your itinerary, checks live NATHNAC guidance, and builds a personalised vaccination and health plan.

3 Vaccinate

Vaccines administered

Vaccines are given on the same visit where possible. Our pharmacists are trained in managing reactions and follow strict cold-chain protocols.

4 Travel

Travel with confidence

Leave with your vaccination record, ICVP certificate if required, antimalarial prescription, and a destination-specific health briefing.
