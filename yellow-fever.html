What you need to know

What is Yellow Fever?

Yellow Fever is a serious viral haemorrhagic disease caused by the Yellow Fever virus — a Flavivirus spread by infected Aedes and Haemagogus mosquitoes. The name comes from the jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes) that affects some patients.

It is endemic in tropical regions of sub-Saharan Africa and South America. The disease cannot be treated once contracted — only the symptoms can be managed — making vaccination the only reliable form of protection.

A single dose of the Yellow Fever vaccine provides lifelong immunity in over 99% of recipients and is effective from 10 days after vaccination.

How is it transmitted?

Yellow Fever is transmitted exclusively by the bite of an infected mosquito — it cannot spread person-to-person. Aedes mosquitoes bite during the day, making standard malaria precautions (which focus on night-time biting) insufficient on their own.

Understanding your ICVP certificate

The International Certificate of Vaccination or Prophylaxis is the only document accepted at borders as proof of Yellow Fever vaccination. Here's everything you need to know about it.

Only designated centres can issue it

The ICVP can only be issued by an NHS-approved Yellow Fever Vaccination Centre. A vaccine administered anywhere else — including non-designated pharmacies or private GPs — cannot be certificated. Southdowns Pharmacy is an approved issuing centre at all four Hampshire branches.

Valid from 10 days after vaccination

The certificate is not valid immediately. It becomes active 10 days after the date of your vaccination. This is the time required for your immune system to generate full protection. Book at least 10 days before departure — ideally much earlier.

Valid for life — no renewal needed

Since July 2016, the WHO updated the IHR to recognise that a single Yellow Fever vaccination provides lifelong immunity. Certificates issued under the old 10-year rule have also been extended. You will never need a booster — unless you were immunocompromised at the time of your original vaccination.

Keep it safe — it cannot easily be replaced

The physical ICVP booklet is the legally accepted document. If you lose it, obtaining a replacement requires re-vaccination to generate a new certificate — there is no central registry. Photograph your certificate and store the image securely as a backup.

Had the vaccine elsewhere but lost your certificate?

Unfortunately we cannot issue a replacement certificate for a vaccination given at another centre. The ICVP must be issued by the centre that administered the vaccine. If you no longer have your certificate, you will need to be re-vaccinated to obtain a new one.

Who can receive it

Eligibility for the Yellow Fever vaccine

Yellow Fever is a live attenuated vaccine — meaning it contains a weakened form of the virus. This makes it highly effective but means it's not suitable for everyone.

Generally suitable

  • Adults and children aged 9 months and over

    The minimum age for safe administration

  • Healthy adults with no contraindications

    Including those with minor, well-controlled conditions

  • Travellers to endemic or certificate-required countries

    Whether the certificate is mandatory or recommended

  • Those with a mild egg intolerance (not allergy)

    Discuss with pharmacist — different to egg allergy

  • Individuals requiring a replacement ICVP record

    Re-vaccination needed if original certificate is lost

Contraindicated

  • Infants under 6 months

    High risk of vaccine-associated encephalitis

  • Severe allergy to eggs, chicken, or gelatin

    Vaccine is grown on embryonated eggs

  • Severely immunocompromised individuals

    e.g. active chemotherapy, HIV with CD4 <200, organ transplant

  • Thymus disease (thymoma, myasthenia gravis)

    Associated with risk of severe adverse reaction

  • Previous severe reaction to Yellow Fever vaccine

    Including anaphylaxis or neurological reaction

Needs individual assessment — speak to our pharmacist

Pregnancy

Generally avoided due to theoretical risk to the foetus. However, if travel to a high-risk area is unavoidable, vaccination may be recommended — the risk of Yellow Fever can outweigh the vaccine risk. We will assess this with you individually.

Aged 60 or over

Older adults face a higher risk of vaccine-associated viscerotropic disease (YEL-AVD) — a rare but serious side effect. Our pharmacists will conduct a thorough benefit-risk assessment and may issue a medical exemption waiver if vaccination is inadvisable.

Breastfeeding

Generally avoided unless travel to a high-risk area is essential and cannot be postponed. There is a small theoretical risk of transmission of the vaccine virus through breast milk to the infant. Discuss with our pharmacist before your appointment.

Can't be vaccinated but need to travel? In some cases, our pharmacist can issue a medical exemption waiver letter — an official document accepted by some (not all) countries in place of the ICVP. Book a consultation to discuss your options.

Yellow Fever vaccination FAQs

Answers to the questions we're asked most often.

The Yellow Fever vaccination at Southdowns Pharmacy costs £65 per dose. This price includes the travel health consultation, the Stamaril® vaccine, and the official ICVP certificate — there are no hidden charges. The Yellow Fever vaccine is a private service and is not available free on the NHS.
Walk-ins are welcome for travel health advice, but we recommend booking an appointment for vaccinations to guarantee vaccine stock availability and minimise your wait. Same-day appointments are often available — call your nearest branch to check. Booking online ensures everything is ready for you when you arrive.
You must be vaccinated at least 10 days before departure — the ICVP certificate only becomes valid 10 days after vaccination. If you travel within that 10-day window, your certificate will not be accepted at the border. Ideally book 4–6 weeks in advance to allow time and reduce any last-minute stress.
No — in the vast majority of cases, you do not need a booster. Since July 2016, the WHO confirmed that a single Yellow Fever dose provides lifelong protection. All previously issued 10-year certificates have also been automatically extended for life. The only exceptions are individuals who were immunocompromised at the time of their original vaccination, or those vaccinated as infants under 2 years old — these groups may need re-vaccination. Bring your original ICVP to your appointment and our pharmacist will confirm your status.
Yes, for children aged 9 months and over. The vaccine is not recommended for infants under 6 months due to the risk of vaccine-associated encephalitis, and should only be considered for infants aged 6–8 months if travel to a high-risk area is unavoidable. For children aged 9 months and above, the vaccine is safe and effective. Please bring a parent or guardian to the appointment.
Most people experience only mild, short-lived side effects: soreness at the injection site, mild headache, low-grade fever, and fatigue for a few days after vaccination. These resolve on their own. Serious adverse reactions are very rare — our pharmacists are trained to manage reactions on-site, and you'll be asked to remain for a brief observation period after vaccination. Inform us in advance of any allergies or medical conditions.
Yes. Inactivated vaccines such as Typhoid, Hepatitis A, and Hepatitis B can safely be given alongside the Yellow Fever vaccine at the same appointment. Other live vaccines (such as MMR) should ideally be given on the same day or separated by at least 4 weeks. Our pharmacist will review your full vaccination schedule and advise on the optimal timing for each vaccine.
If you have a genuine medical contraindication, our pharmacist can issue a medical exemption waiver letter — an official document that explains why vaccination was clinically inadvisable. Some (not all) countries accept this in lieu of the ICVP. It's important to check with your destination's embassy before travelling, as not all borders accept exemption letters. Book a consultation so we can assess your specific situation and advise on the best course of action.
