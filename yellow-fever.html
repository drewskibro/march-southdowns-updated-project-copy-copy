What you need to know

What is Yellow Fever?

Yellow Fever is a serious viral haemorrhagic disease caused by the Yellow Fever virus — a Flavivirus spread by infected Aedes and Haemagogus mosquitoes. The name comes from the jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes) that affects some patients.

It is endemic in tropical regions of sub-Saharan Africa and South America. The disease cannot be treated once contracted — only the symptoms can be managed — making vaccination the only reliable form of protection.

A single dose of the Yellow Fever vaccine provides lifelong immunity in over 99% of recipients and is effective from 10 days after vaccination.

How is it transmitted?

Yellow Fever is transmitted exclusively by the bite of an infected mosquito — it cannot spread person-to-person. Aedes mosquitoes bite during the day, making standard malaria precautions (which focus on night-time biting) insufficient on their own.

Conditional

Required if arriving from an endemic country

Some countries only require a Yellow Fever certificate if you've recently been in a Yellow Fever-endemic region. This includes travellers transiting through endemic airports for 12+ hours. A multi-destination itinerary through Africa or South America can trigger this requirement.

Examples include

India Thailand Indonesia Australia China Egypt
Recommended

Recommended — no certificate required, but risk is real

Some endemic countries don't require a certificate but the disease is present. NATHNAC recommends vaccination to protect your health — even if no one will ask to see your ICVP at the border. Better protected than not.

Examples include

Brazil Peru Kenya Tanzania Ecuador Colombia

Understanding your ICVP certificate

The International Certificate of Vaccination or Prophylaxis is the only document accepted at borders as proof of Yellow Fever vaccination. Here's everything you need to know about it.

Only designated centres can issue it

The ICVP can only be issued by an NHS-approved Yellow Fever Vaccination Centre. A vaccine administered anywhere else — including non-designated pharmacies or private GPs — cannot be certificated. Southdowns Pharmacy is an approved issuing centre at all four Hampshire branches.

Valid from 10 days after vaccination

The certificate is not valid immediately. It becomes active 10 days after the date of your vaccination. This is the time required for your immune system to generate full protection. Book at least 10 days before departure — ideally much earlier.

Valid for life — no renewal needed

Since July 2016, the WHO updated the IHR to recognise that a single Yellow Fever vaccination provides lifelong immunity. Certificates issued under the old 10-year rule have also been extended. You will never need a booster — unless you were immunocompromised at the time of your original vaccination.

Keep it safe — it cannot easily be replaced

The physical ICVP booklet is the legally accepted document. If you lose it, obtaining a replacement requires re-vaccination to generate a new certificate — there is no central registry. Photograph your certificate and store the image securely as a backup.

Had the vaccine elsewhere but lost your certificate?

Unfortunately we cannot issue a replacement certificate for a vaccination given at another centre. The ICVP must be issued by the centre that administered the vaccine. If you no longer have your certificate, you will need to be re-vaccinated to obtain a new one.
